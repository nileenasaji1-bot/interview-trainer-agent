ROLE: Data Analyst (Entry to Mid Level)

=== TECHNICAL QUESTIONS ===

Q1: What is the difference between JOIN types in SQL (INNER, LEFT, RIGHT, FULL)?
A: INNER JOIN returns only matching rows from both tables. LEFT JOIN returns
all rows from the left table plus matches from the right (NULL if no match).
RIGHT JOIN is the reverse. FULL JOIN returns all rows from both tables.
Follow-up: Write a query to find customers who have never placed an order.

Q2: How do you handle missing data in a dataset?
A: Options include: deletion (if missing data is small %), mean/median/mode
imputation, forward/backward fill for time series, or predictive imputation
using other features. The right choice depends on why the data is missing
and how much is missing.
Follow-up: What's the risk of always using mean imputation?

Q3: Explain the difference between correlation and causation.
A: Correlation means two variables move together statistically. Causation
means one variable directly influences the other. Correlation does not
imply causation - a third confounding variable can cause both.
Follow-up: Give an example of a spurious correlation.

Q4: What is A/B testing and how do you determine statistical significance?
A: A/B testing compares two versions (control vs variant) to measure the
effect of a change. Statistical significance is typically assessed using a
p-value (commonly p < 0.05) from a hypothesis test, indicating the observed
difference is unlikely due to random chance.
Follow-up: What's a Type I vs Type II error in this context?

Q5: What visualization would you use to show a trend over time vs a
comparison across categories?
A: Line charts for trends over time (continuous progression). Bar charts
for comparing discrete categories. Avoid pie charts for more than 4-5
categories - they're hard to compare precisely.
Follow-up: When would a box plot be more useful than a bar chart?

=== BEHAVIORAL QUESTIONS ===

Q6: Tell me about a time your analysis changed a business decision.
Tip: Focus on the business impact, not just the technical method. Quantify
the outcome if possible (e.g. "reduced churn by X%").

Q7: Describe a time you had to present complex data to a non-technical audience.
Tip: Emphasize simplification without losing accuracy - use analogies,
visuals, and lead with the "so what" before the methodology.

Q8: Tell me about a time you found an error in your own analysis after
sharing it.
Tip: Show accountability and the process you used to catch and correct it,
plus what you changed to prevent it happening again.

=== GENERAL TIPS ===
- Be ready to walk through your resume projects in SQL/Python/Excel detail.
- Practice explaining a p-value in plain English - it comes up often.
- Bring a portfolio project you can screen-share and explain end to end.
